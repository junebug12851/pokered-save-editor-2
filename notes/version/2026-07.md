### 2026-07-11 -- Exchange prices the WHOLE trade, not each step (no more phantom refunds)

Fixed the exchange economics. The trade was priced **per step** — each item you asked for was
separately rounded up to a whole number of the item you were paying with, and each one handed back
its own leftover as money. That massively overcharged and over-refunded: asking for 3 Fresh Water
(₽200 each) took **3** Potions (₽300 each) and refunded ₽300, when 3 Fresh Water is ₽600, which is
**exactly 2 Potions and no change at all**.

Now the whole trade is priced **once**: the total value being bought is rounded up to a whole number
of the given item, and only that single leftover comes back as money. A trade that divides evenly
costs nothing extra and **refunds nothing** — and the rate line under the button drops the "+₽0"
entirely rather than showing a refund that isn't happening. Verified live: 3 Awakening (₽600) from
Antidotes (₽100) now reads exactly `-6 Antidote` with no refund and money unchanged; 3 Fresh Water
from Super Potions (₽700) takes 1 Super Potion + ₽100 back instead of the old 3 + ₽1,500.

The rate lines also now describe the **queued** trade rather than a fictional per-unit rate, so what's
written on Checkout is literally what the card shows. `perAGive`/`perARefund`/`perBGive`/`perBRefund`
are replaced by `giveFor(dir, steps)` / `refundFor(dir, steps)`, which the preview, the "+" gating,
and `checkout()` all share — one pricing path, so they cannot disagree. Money still only ever goes up.
`ctest` green (72/72).

### 2026-07-11 -- Exchange: give what you have, get anything (+ Potion <-> Fresh Water default)

Reworked the item Exchange dropdowns (Healing / Custom) so you're no longer boxed in by what's
already in your bag. The two sides are now **asymmetric**: the **left** side is what you **give**, so
it lists the items you actually **own**; the **right** side is what you **get**, so it lists **every**
exchangeable item — owned or not. You can now trade a stack of Antidotes into a Fresh Water you've
never carried.

To keep that honest, the get-side list **greys out (and blocks) the items your stock can't cover** —
with 25 Antidotes (₽2,500) the list shows Calcium, Carbos and Full Restore dimmed, because no whole
number of Antidotes buys even one of them. That greying is also what **guarantees the two "+" buttons
are never both disabled**: whatever you *can* select is by definition affordable, so the "get" side
always has a live "+". (Once you've taken a step, the opposite "+" comes back too, so you can always
walk it back.)

The **Healing** tab now opens on **Potion ⇄ Fresh Water**. The give side prefers the potion family in
order — Potion, Super Potion, Hyper Potion, Max Potion, Full Restore, then the rest — picking the best
one you actually have (so a save with 8 Super Potions and no Potions opens on Super Potion, never on
Antidote); the get side prefers Fresh Water. If you own nothing in the category the give list falls
back to showing everything, so the dropdown is never empty.

New `ItemExchangeModel` API: `sourceItems()` / `targetItems()` (replacing `ownedItems()`),
`canGainTarget()`, and `pickDefaults()`, plus a `revision` counter the QML `model:` bindings depend on
so the lists — and their affordability flags — rebuild whenever counts or money move (the list getters
are invokables, which a binding otherwise has nothing to re-run on). Verified live over the debug
channel on a real save. No save bytes change. `tst_qml_screens` green.

### 2026-07-10 -- Market Exchange sub-tabs: Currency / Healing / Custom (item<->item trading)

The Market's **Exchange** tab now has a sub-tab bar. **Currency** is the existing money<->coins
converter (unchanged, just moved under the tab). **Healing** and **Custom** are new: they trade one item
for another. Each side is an item dropdown (owned, non-zero, exchangeable items — Healing filters to
healing/drink items, Custom lists everything you own) with the item's count and a **+** button; the two
lists exclude each other's pick so you can't trade an item for itself.

**The trade:** each "+X" step gains ONE of that item and draws the **minimum whole number of the other
item whose BUY value covers it**, refunding the leftover value as **money** (money only ever goes up).
So with Antidote (₽100) and Burn Heal (₽250): **+Antidote** takes 1 Burn Heal and refunds ₽150;
**+Burn Heal** takes 3 Antidotes and refunds ₽50. Steps accumulate, the after-counts and money are
previewed live, and **nothing is written until Checkout** (which routes to the item exchange on those
sub-tabs). Each **+** disables independently the moment *its* direction becomes impossible — not enough
of the source item, no room for the gained item, or money would exceed ₽999,999 — leaving the other side
usable, exactly like the Currency tab's buttons.

New `ItemExchangeModel` (`brg.itemExchangeModel`) drives both item sub-tabs off a single net axis (the
same shape as the money<->coins coin axis); it reads/writes items across the **bag + PC storage combined**
(consuming and adding bag-first, then storage). Backed by new `ItemStorageBox` helpers —
`capacityForInd()` / `addAmount()` / `removeAmount()` (stack to 99, spill into new rows, drain and drop
emptied rows). Values come from each item's money **buy price** (the price players see in-game).
`tst_qml_screens` green.

Also added non-visual automation hooks for the debug channel (`marketPage.debugExchangeSub`, `exAddA`/
`exAddB`) so the harness can drive the Exchange sub-tabs without simulating clicks.

### 2026-07-10 -- Hot-reload now keeps you on the screen you're editing (debug harness)

Improved the DEBUG-only QML hot-reload so live iteration doesn't fight you. A reload rebuilds the whole
QML tree from the root (there's no safe per-file partial reload in a single-`QQuickWidget` app — the
engine caches whole compiled components, so a partial swap would risk stale copies of shared components),
which previously dumped you back at Home + the New File modal on every save. `MainWindow::reloadQml()` now
records the current screen first (reverse-looking-up the top of `Router::stack` to its registered name)
and, after the reload re-seats App.qml's startup stack, dismisses the New File modal and navigates back to
that screen (`pokemonDetails` re-opens party mon 0). The loaded save was already preserved (it lives in
C++, not QML), so you keep your save and stay on the screen with changes applied. Home and modals are
intentionally not auto-restored. Verified over the debug TCP channel (`title`→`reload`→`title` returns the
same screen). DEBUG-only — release builds are unaffected. See `notes/reference/dev-harness.md`.

### 2026-07-09 -- Resolved the type-2 "single truth": single types write duplicate-of-type1

Settled the last tracked type-2 decision straight from the pokered disassembly. A single-type
Pokémon is stored as a **duplicate of type 1** (`data/pokemon/base_stats/*.asm` — e.g. Charmander is
`db FIRE, FIRE`), `0xFF` is not a valid type at all (`constants/type_constants.asm` runs `$00..$1A`),
and `engine/pokemon/add_mon.asm` copies both type bytes verbatim from the base-stats header into
party/box data. So the game's — and now the editor's — canonical written form for a single type is
duplicate-of-type1.

`save()` already emitted that for a non-explicit single type, so on-disk output is unchanged for
normal saves; **byte fidelity is untouched** — a save that literally stored `0xFF` (only ever a
hacked/glitch save) still round-trips as `0xFF` via `type2Explicit`. The real fix: every editor
(re)generation path — `randomize()`, `correctTypes()`, and `update(resetType)` — now clears
`type2Explicit`, so a *generated or corrected* single type serialises as the duplicate rather than a
stale `0xFF`. `isCorrected()`'s tolerance (accept either `0xFF` or the duplicate as a valid single
type) is now the finalized, intended behaviour — the "temporary dirty patch" framing is gone. New
regression test `box_singleTypeCanonicalForm` pins it; the load/save/isCorrected comments and
`reference/gen1-knowledge.md` now document the resolved truth.

### 2026-07-06 -- Adopted the updated fairyfox docs-site chrome + reader menu

Refreshed the read-only hub clone (0.11.2 → 0.14.4) and adopted the hub's updated **docs-site**
standard into the Doxygen theme, mirroring how the sibling `random-ai-prompt` did it (adapted from
its JSDoc stack to Doxygen — a faithful hand-reimplementation, the standard's intended model). The
standing `adopt-standards-by-default` authorization covered it, so it applied without the
report-then-wait pause; full verification still ran (local Doxygen 1.17 build + live in-browser
check across dark / light / sepia, API vs prose pages, and the reader).

What changed in `docs/fairyfox/`:

- **Chrome** — replaced the old brand bar + "Back to Fairy Fox" button with the current mesh chrome:
  a fixed primary nav (Home · Projects · Games · Docs · Updates · About) with the **hub fox logo** as
  the way home, and a project subnav (Overview · Notes · Classes · Files · Screenshots · Repository ·
  Notes). **Projects** is the active primary item for a project's docs. Footer redesigned to the
  standard's brand · Explore · This project columns + bottom bar.
- **Reader menu** (`ff-docs.js`, new) — the "Aa" reading-settings panel: theme (Light / Sepia / Dark
  + Auto), accent swatches, text-size slider, line-spacing and width, saved under the shared,
  origin-wide key `fairyfox:reader:b`. It also drives doxygen-awesome's `light-mode`/`dark-mode`
  class (the reader is now the single theme authority), measures `--ff-header-h`, and marks the
  active subnav item.
- **API-only sidebar** — the treeview shows only on the class/file/namespace API pages; Overview, the
  notes/markdown pages render full-width prose (like the sibling). A guard freezes `doxygen_width` on
  prose pages so the hidden sidebar can't corrupt the API-page sidebar width.
- **Theme-conflict fix** — the token mapping is now scoped to all three of doxygen-awesome's theme
  states so our fairyfox tokens win over its `html:not(.light-mode)` / `html.dark-mode` dark rules
  (previously Light mode on a dark OS washed the text out to doxygen-awesome's dark foreground).
  `HTML_COLORSTYLE` moved `AUTO_DARK → LIGHT` so Doxygen stops injecting its own competing dark
  script. Added the `data-theme` light + sepia palettes.
- **Self-hosted fonts** — Fraunces / Inter / JetBrains Mono woff2 + `fonts.css` under
  `docs/fairyfox/fonts/` (no Google Fonts; removes the third-party IP exposure). `pages.yml` no
  longer injects DoxygenLayout user-tabs — the subnav carries those links.

**Follow-up polish (same day), from a visual review in Chrome against the reference site:** hid
doxygen-awesome's own `#titlearea` + `#main-menu` (they duplicated our chrome's nav and, with its
fixed layout, produced a second scrollbar); switched to a single-page scroll (content flows; the
treeview is a fixed column on API pages, absent on prose pages); and made the reader's accent links
**theme-aware and vivid** (the old formula mixed the accent toward the near-white body text, washing
links out to a muddy pastel on dark — now it lightens on dark / darkens on light, keeping saturation).
Note: this also hides Doxygen's built-in search box (it lived in `#main-menu`), matching the reference
site's chrome.

Docs-site only — no app code changed, so **no `VERSION` bump**; under git-flow this rides to `main`
with the next real release rather than merging on its own. Process report:
`notes/fairyfox-reports/2026-07-06-adopting-updates.md`.
